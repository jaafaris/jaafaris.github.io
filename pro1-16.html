Prediction of the Age, Gender, and Facial Expressions of Humans from Images

University of Tehran

In the digital age, especially post-Covid-19, virtual interactions through platforms such as Zoom, Skype, and other video conferencing tools have become essential for communication. These live-streamed video interactions offer an abundant, untapped data source for various predictive analytics. This project aims to leverage webcam data to predict age, gender, and emotional expressionâ€”insights that can empower industries like sales and customer service by providing a deeper understanding of client profiles and engagement patterns. For example, sales representatives could use these insights to tailor their interactions based on real-time customer feedback.

The core objective of our project was to develop a robust model that accurately predicts age, gender, and facial expressions from webcam images. Using sophisticated feature extraction techniques, we created a comprehensive feature vector for each image, providing the foundation for effective classification. The ability to analyze facial expressions adds a unique dimension, enabling real-time emotional feedback that enhances customer interaction strategies.

The data pre-processing phase was critical to model accuracy. We implemented a multi-step approach, including data cleaning to remove noise, data editing to ensure consistency, and data reduction to streamline processing. With a refined dataset, we applied and tested several machine learning algorithms, including Support Vector Machines (SVM), Decision Trees, and regression models. Through rigorous evaluation, we identified the models that delivered the highest performance across age, gender, and emotion classification, ensuring accuracy and responsiveness.
